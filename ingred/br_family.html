Order Brassicales - Family Tree

The Order Brassicales contains a huge number of very diverse plants. One family alone, Brassicaceae, contains over 350 genera and some 3000 species, many of which have been important in human survival and are still mafor vegetables in our markets. Some others are decoratives and others just plain weeds. I have listed here only families, genera and species that are significant for human consumption.

Some species within Brassicaceae show tremendous variation with different "cultivars" within the species appearing to be completely different plants. This variation is the result of human tampering over thousands of years, breeding separate lineages of the same plant to emphasize particular desirable characteristics.
