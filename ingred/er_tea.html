Camellia sinensis Teas

The making of tea from leaves of the Camellia sinensis apparently began, probably as a medicinal tea, in Yunan, in the far southwestern corner of China, a few more than 3000 years ago. It is said to have spread to the rest of ]China after the State of Qin conquered Sichuan, just to the north of Yunan. Translation of words in early documments is uncertain, so the first unequivicable written record we have for tea drinking is from around 300 BCE.   Photo by Eric Chan W.C. distributed under license Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike v3.0 Unported.
