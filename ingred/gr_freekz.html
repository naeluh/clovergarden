Freekeh


[Farik; Firik (Turk); Triticum turgidum var. durum]

Green (unripe) wheat is roasted to give it an attractive smoky flavor. It's quite a bit more expensive than Bulgur. This form is used mainly in the Levant, Egypt, and across North Africa, but now (2019) it is widely available in Turkey due to the influx of Sirian refugees, It has recently been discovered by North American health food enthusiasts. The samples we have used were imported from Syria and Jordan.

Traditionally, this was made by burning the wheat in its husks, controlled so the damp kernel is not burned. It is then rubbed to rid it of the char, dried, and usually cracked. All the samples I have seen are very coarsely crushed as in the photo, or just a little finer. This grain is relatively perishable and should be used soon after purchase.

More on Wheat.

Buying:

  Freekeh can be found packaged in markets serving a Levantine community.

Storing:

  This product is relatively perishable and subject to rancidity. In a tightly sealed container in a cool place it will be usable up to 4 months.

Cooking:

  Moderately cracked Freekeh takes 45 minutes to an hour of wet cooking to expand and become reasonably tender. Finely cracked (depending on brand) takes less time. for cooking you need water 2 to 2-1/2 times the volume of Freekeh to be cooked.
