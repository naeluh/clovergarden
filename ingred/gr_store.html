Grain, Seed & Flour Storage

The storability of grains, seeds, nuts and flours varies tremendously. This page presents a chart showing approximately how long these various items can be safely stored after purchase and under different conditions. Of necessity the given times are approximate because they are dependent on exact conditions of storage and how long they have already been stored by time you buy them. You should develop the ability to detect rancidity by smell and trust your nose.

General Notes

  1. Vitamin Loss:

      Some of the grains and seeds are listed with very long storage times, but vitamin loss is very significant by 3 years and vitamins are essentially gone by 5 years.

  2. Beans:

      Technically beans will keep for many years without spoiling - BUT - in practice a year from purchase is about maximum (they're probably at least 9 months old by time they get on the store shelf). Beans that have been stored too long simply will not soften in cooking, not even long cooking. They may still be edible, but not pleasantly so.

  3. Storage Conditions

    • Sealed:   This means stored very dry in a tightly sealed fairly full container kept away from sunlight and in as cool and dry a place as you can manage. Any compromise on these conditions will significantly reduce storage life.
    • Fridge:   Packed very dry in an airtight bag or other sealed container and maintained between 32° and 40°F (0° and 4 °C).
    • Freezer:   Packed very dry in an airtight bag and kept frozen at less than 10°F (-12° C).

  4. Rancidity:

      Not only does rancidity taste terrible and produce unpleasant odors, products of rancidity are suspected carcenogens, so consumption of rancid products should be avoided.

  5. White Rice:

      Storage depends on milling, 1 yr to 10+ years. Give it the "sniff test" for rancidity.
