Do-Ahead   -   (1-1/4 hrs - 15 min work)
  1. Peel YUCA fairly deep to remove wax coating, skin, and all color from under the skin.
  2. Cut into the lengths you want your sticks to be, usually 2-1/2 to 3 inches.
  3. Place in a pot with water to cover well (they will expand). Bring to a boil and set to a simmer until done, about 35 minutes. There should be no hard opaque white areas in them. They will break up lengthwise by time they're done.
  4. Drain and let cool. break in half lengthwise and pull the hard root out of the center. Cut into sticks as close to 1/2 inch square as you can get (mostly not real close).
  5. Spread out on a baking sheet or similar, and set in the freezer for 6 hours, or as long as many days.
Make   -   (30 min)
  1. Do not thaw the sticks.
  2. Place Oil in a kadhai, wok or other suitable deep fry container. Bring up to about 375°F/190°C and fry Sticks in modest size batches - not too dark, they will look a bit darker as they cool.
  3. Scoop out with you spider and drain on paper towels.
  4. While still warm, tumble with very fine Salt (see Salt).
  5. Serve, warm or at room temperature, with dip of your choice.
NOTES:

  1. Yuca:

      [Cassava, Manioc, Tapioca root]   This root is sold in any market serving a Tropical or Subtropical community. It is heavily waxed to prevent drying out, but is still highly perishable. Buy from a market with heavy turnover and check there are no soft spots. As it ages, Yuca gets purple streaks in the flesh. I always buy an extra, in case one is disappointing. For details see our Cassava / Manioc / Yuca page.

  2. Salt:

      I run regular sea salt crystals in my spice grinder so the powder adheres to the Yuca sticks better.

  3. Dips:

      There are many South American dips, and they may cross borders freely. Here are two examples (Peru) Rocoto Sauce / Dip / (Brazil) Yogurt Mint Sauce / Dip. You can use just a Salt-Vinegar dip, or most Southeast Asian dips.

  4. Comments:

      These are best served immediately, but are pretty durable. The outside is still crisp after several hours at room temperature, but eventually become just stiff.
