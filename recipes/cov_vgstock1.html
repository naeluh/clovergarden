Soybeans Sprout & Mushroom

This is the most complex of these stocks, and takes the longest, but it is my favorite. It does taste of soybean sprouts, of course. Some people use only Soybean Sprouts, and some omit the Bamboo Shoots. Makes 2-1/4 quarts.

  1. In a 3 quart saucepan, place Dried Shiitakes and 2-1/2 quarts (10 cups) of Water. Allow to soak for 15 minutes or more.
  2. Slice Bamboo Shoots (if using) fairly thin. Use vacuum packed shoots, not canned.
  3. Stir in Bean Sprouts and Bamboo Shoots. Bring to a boil and simmer slowly for about 40 minutes.
  4. Strain out solids.

NOTES:

  1. Comments:

      Vegetable stocks such as these are used particularly in Chinese and Vietnamese Buddhist communities - Chinese Buddhism is much more strict about vegetarianism than Thai Buddhism, which is a bit more "don't ask, don't tell". All these stocks are based on those given by über expert Fuchsia Dunlop. You should pick the one that will go best with your other ingredients.
