Do-Ahead   -   (8 hrs - 5 min work)
  1. IF using dried: soak BEANS with 1 cup water and 1 teaspoon Salt for 8 to 10 hours (see Note-4).
Prep   -   (30 min)
  1. Chop ONIONS small.
  2. Crush GARLIC and chop small.
  3. Peel CARROTS and chop small. Chop CELERY small. Mix.
  4. Dry pan roast Cumin Seed until aromatic. Dry pan roast Coriander Seed until aromatic. Cool both and grind in your spice grinder along with Allspice.
  5. Squeeze LEMON JUICE.
  6. Chop CHIVES for Garnish.
Run   -   (1-1/4 hr)
  1. In a heavy bottomed saucepan (3-1/2 quart), heat Oil and fry Onions, stirring until translucent.
  2. Stir in Garlic and Spices mix. Fry stirring for another minute.
  3. Stir in Carrot mix and fry stirring another minute.
  4. Stir in Freekeh, 4 cups Stock, and 2 cups Water. Bring to a boil, cover and turn to a simmer for 45 minutes to an hour, or until Freekeh is tender.
  5. Stir in Beans according to your best judgement as to how much more cooking they can take, and the texture you want.
  6. Season with 1/2 T Salt (or to taste if your Stock was salty) and 1/2 t Pepper.
  7. Stir in Olive Oil ExtV and Lemon Juice. Take off the heat.
  8. Ladle into bowls and garnish with a big blob of Yogurt and a sprinkle of Chives. If serving buffet style, provide the Yogurt in a bowl with a spoon to be added by guests as desired.
NOTES:

  1. Beans:

      The pattern recipe called for Pinto Beans, which were used in the photo example - white beans will give a lighter color soup. Use whatever beans you like - common beans aren't native to the Levant, so authenticity is not an issue. Soaking with salt improves the beans. See also Note-4.

  2. Freekeh:

      This is immature Wheat, roasted over wood. It can be found in markets serving a Levantine community. For details see our Freekeh page.

  3. Stock:

      The pattern recipe called for Chicken or Vegetable stock. You can use the bean cooking liquid as part of the stock if you wish. You can fake up a stock using Chicken Powder or Vegeta if you have none on hand.

  4. Comments:

      The pattern recipe used canned Pinto Beans. Using a good brand of canned beans would cut the recipe time to 1-3/4 hours, and wouldn't make a lot of difference.
