Yes		 This standard Thai ingredient is both a thickener and flavoring. It is available commercially in small shaker jars, but it's fresher made at home.

Sweet Rice (1)  
Make:   -   (15 min)
  1. In an iron pan dry roast RICE over moderate heat, stirring almost constantly until it has about the color of brown rice, then cool thoroughly and grind fine in your spice grinder.
  2. Kept in a tightly sealed jar in a cool place away from light it should be good for 6 months.
NOTES:

  1. Sweet Rice:

      Also known as "glutinous rice" (it contains no gluten) and "sticky rice", this chalky looking rice is available in just about all East and Southeast Asian markets. It's usually packed in smaller bags because (except in Laos and the Laotian part of northern Thailand) it's used mainly for sweets. There are two varieties, Thai Long Grain, and Short Grain everywhere else. Either can be used.
