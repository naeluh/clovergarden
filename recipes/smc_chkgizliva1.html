Yes		 In Southeast Asia tough chicken gizzards and tender livers are often combined in the same dish - cooking the gizzards long and the livers short. This savory adobo is one of my favorites.

Make:   -   (2-1/4 hrs)
  1. Wash GIZZARDS and cut into pieces, about 4 pieces per full size gizzard. Put them in a pot, cover well with water, bring to a boil and simmer for 30 minutes. Drain and rinse. Clean pot and return gizzards to it.
  2. Crush GARLIC and chop small. Quarter ONION lengthwise and slice crosswise about 1/8 inch thick. Add all Adobo items to the pot.
  3. Bring to a boil and simmer for about 1-1/4 hours until gizzards are tender. Regulate liquid so there is just enough left to cook the livers when the gizzards are done.
  4. Wash LIVERS, cut into chunks similar to the gizzards. Stir into the pot. Cover and simmer about 10 minutes stirring once or twice.
  5. Serve warm with plenty of steamed Jasmine rice.
NOTES:

  1. Vinegar:

      Filipinos are really big on vinegar and they have some good ones (palm, cane, coconut). I prefer a natural dark Sukang Iloco by Datu Puti (only ingredient - naturally fermented sugar cane juice. Other good Philippine vinegars can be used. For details see our Sours page.
