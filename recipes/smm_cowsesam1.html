Prep   -   (40 min - 15 min work)
  1. Cut BEEF across the grain into narrow strips less than 1/4 inch thick.
  2. Mix all Marinade items and massage into Beef. Set aside for at least half an hour.
  3. Crush GARLIC and chop fine. Chop Thai Chilis fine. Mix.
  4. Mix Sesame Paste, Stock, Salt and Pepper.
  5. Cut Red Chilis into very narrow strips for garnish.
Run   -   (10 min)
  1. In a spacious sauté pan or wok, heat OIL and fry Garlic mix over moderate flame until garlic starts to color. Quickly stir in Beef and turn flame up high. Fry stirring until all liquid has evaporated and it's actually frying.
  2. Stir in Sesame Paste mix and fry stirring until beef is well coated (don't dally or the sauce will start sticking to the pan).
  3. Garnish with Chili Strips and serve hot with Lime Wedges and plenty of Jasmine Rice.
NOTES:

  1. Beef

      Weight is with all excess fat removed. On the other hand, beef with too little fat lacks flavor, there should be a little marbled through it.

  2. Chilis

      Thai, de Arbol or other quite to very hot chilis, red or green. For details see our Thai Chilis page.

  3. Sesame Paste

      Easily available Tahini (Middle Eastern sesame paste) can be substituted for scarce Southeast Asian paste. Tahini is made from hulled sesame so is less bitter than Asian.

  4. Chilis

      Red chilis - around here we all use Red Fresnos, but other medium or mild chilis can be used. For details see our Thai Chilis page.
