Yes		 An excellent side, appetizer, or vegetarian main dish. This recipe is similar to the canned "Giant White Beans" from Bulgaria and Turkey, but costs less and tastes better.

DoAhead   -   (9-1/2 hrs - 5 min work)
  1. Soak LIMA BEANS for at least 8 hour with water to cover and 1/2 Tablespoon of salt.
  2. Drain Lima Beans and place in a saucepan with water to cover. Bring to a boil and simmer for around 30 minutes - they will not be fully cooked at this time. Drain and set aside.
Prep   -   (45 min)
  1. Chop ONION fine. Crush GARLIC and chop fine. Peel CARROTS and chop fine. Chop CELERY small. Chop PARSLEY small. Mix all.
  2. Chop TOMATOES small. Chop Dill fine. Mix all Tomato mix items, including the juice from the tomatoes.
Run   -   (1-1/4 hr or more)
  1. In a coverable sauté pan (3-1/2 quart) heat Olive Oil and fry Onion mix until Onions are translucent, but not browning.
  2. Stir in Tomato mix. Cover tightly and simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Stir in Lima Beans. Simmer covered until Lima Beans are tender, about 30 minutes if quite fresh, up to a couple of hours if older. Check water frequently and add boiling water as needed - it must not get too dry.
  4. Serve hot as a main dish or side dish, or at room temperature as an appetizer. This recipe goes well with rice or bread, and reheats well. If you will serve at room temperature put in a touch more salt, pepper, and lemon juice.
NOTES:

  1. Lima Beans:

      These should be the largest ones you can find. Buy from a source with high turnover - old beans will take a long time or never to cook tender. Yes, soak with salt, see our Soaking / Brining Dried Beans.

  2. Stock:

      The pattern recipe calls for chicken stock, but there's no reason you can't use a good Vegetable Stock to make this dish vegetarian.
